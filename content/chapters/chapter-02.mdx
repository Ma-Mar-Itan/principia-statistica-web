Inference begins with a modest act of ambition: to use what has been observed in order to speak carefully about what has not.
</ChapterLead>

## 2.1 What Is Inference?

Inference is the disciplined movement from the seen to the unseen. It is neither guesswork nor certainty; it is a structured claim about what the data make more or less plausible.

## 2.2 The Role of Probability

Probability gives uncertainty a grammar. It allows us to compare competing claims, update beliefs, and measure how surprising an observation would be under a proposed explanation.

## 2.3 Statistics as Evidence

A statistic is not evidence because it is numerical. It becomes evidence when it is connected to a question, a model, and a standard of comparison.

## 2.4 Beyond the Data

Every inference extends beyond the data that produced it. This extension is powerful, but it is also where assumptions quietly enter the room.

## 2.5 The Limits of Inference

The honest statistician knows that inference has limits. Data can discipline belief, but they cannot remove judgment from decision-making.
